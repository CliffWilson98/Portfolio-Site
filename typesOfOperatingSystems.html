Different types of operating systems

As part of my operating systems course I have learned about the different types of operating systems that exist. Below I will list a few of them along with a brief description. All information found on this page comes from the 4th edition of Modern Operating Systems by Andrew Tanenbaum and Herbet Bos.

Mainframe

Operating systems designed for mainframes are the most powerful of any. These computers are often large enough to fill entire rooms and often have the ability to store millions of gigabytes of data.

Server

Server operating systems are one level down from Mainframes. Servers can serve many users at once and allow for the sharing of resources.

Multiprocessor

Multiprocessor operating systems are designed to take advantage of systems which connect many CPUs in order to increase performance. It is a challenge to allow applications to make use of processing power that is spread across multiple CPUs.

Personal

Personal computer operating systems are the operating systems that most people are familiar with. Common examples are Windows 10, Apple's OS X, and Linux.
